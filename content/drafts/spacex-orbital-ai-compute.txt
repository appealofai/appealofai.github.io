Title: AI data centers in orbit sound absurd until the compute hunger looks ordinary.
Type: Article draft
Date: 2026-06-09
Status: Draft

Angle:
Orbital AI infrastructure sounds like science fiction until the compute problem
starts looking like the least fictional part.

Draft:
AI data centers in space sound ridiculous in the exact way many infrastructure
shifts sound ridiculous right before they become spreadsheets.

The image is almost too clean: solar power, orbital cooling problems, satellites,
chips, launch cadence, a whole machine economy floating above the weather. It
feels absurd. It also feels like a very honest description of where AI is going:
more compute, more energy, more geography.

The appeal is not the spectacle. It is the visibility of the hunger underneath.
AI keeps pretending to be software. Then the infrastructure bill arrives.
